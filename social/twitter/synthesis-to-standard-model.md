1/6 The Standard Model gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) is not one monolithic thing. It is THREE independent gauge modules — color, weak isospin, hypercharge — whose composition produces phenomena absent from any individual piece. New paper on how this assembly works. 🧵

2/6 The compositional keystone is the Higgs mechanism. The SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y symmetry breaks spontaneously to U(1)_EM when the Higgs field acquires its vacuum expectation value v ≈ 246 GeV. Without this step, W and Z bosons are massless and the weak force has infinite range. It doesn't.

3/6 Anomaly cancellation is the most striking emergent constraint. For the quantum theory to be mathematically consistent, the gauge anomalies from quark loops and lepton loops must cancel *exactly*. This forces quarks and leptons to appear in matched sets — they're not independent inputs.

4/6 The renormalization group (RG) is treated as a compositional bridge across energy scales. The three gauge couplings run with energy: g_s (strong) decreases at high energy (asymptotic freedom), g and g' converge toward possible grand unification around 10^15–10^16 GeV.

5/6 Precision tests: m_H ≈ 125.25 GeV at the LHC, electroweak precision observables at LEP/SLC to per-mille accuracy, CKM unitarity tests, neutrino oscillations. The SM passes all of them. The Higgs mass itself remains unexplained — the hierarchy problem persists.
