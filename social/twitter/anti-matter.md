2/6 The universe contains about 1 billion photons for every baryon. That ratio — η ≈ 6.1 × 10⁻¹⁰ — is a fossil of the moment, very early on, when matter won a narrow victory over antimatter. Understanding it requires three things: C violation, CP violation, and departure from thermal equilibrium.

3/6 These are Sakharov's three conditions for baryogenesis (1967). The Standard Model satisfies all three — but by nowhere near enough. The observed baryon asymmetry requires ~10 orders of magnitude more CP violation than the CKM matrix provides. This is a major open problem.
