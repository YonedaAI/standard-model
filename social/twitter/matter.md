1/6 Here's a wild fact: ~99% of your body's mass doesn't come from the Higgs field. It comes from QCD binding energy — quarks zipping around inside protons and neutrons. The Higgs gives quarks their intrinsic mass. Confinement does the rest. New paper explores exactly this. 🧵

2/6 The Standard Model organizes matter into 3 generations of fermions — quarks and leptons — governed by gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1). That's 12 gauge bosons, 3 families of quarks (u/d, c/s, t/b), and 3 families of leptons. A deceptively compact structure for all visible matter.

3/6 Key insight from the paper: the SM is best understood as a *modular composition*, not a monolithic theory. Each layer (Size-Aware → Thermal → Quantum → Gravitational) builds on the last, and the act of composing layers creates genuinely new physics. Emergence is the engine.

4/6 The Higgs mechanism provides mass via spontaneous symmetry breaking — the SU(2)×U(1) symmetry breaks to U(1)_EM at v ≈ 246 GeV. But the top quark at 173 GeV vs the electron at 0.511 MeV — a factor of 340,000 — remains unexplained. The hierarchy problem is open.

5/6 The paper proves three structural theorems: anomaly cancellation (quarks and leptons must come in matched sets), spin-statistics connection, and that CP violation *requires* exactly 3 generations. Three generations is the minimum for the universe to prefer matter over antimatter.
